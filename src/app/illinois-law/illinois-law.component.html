UNDERSTANDING ILLINOIS REAL ESTATE Let’s take a look at what goes into buying a home in Illinois

WORKING WITH ATTORNEYS

Illinois is one of three states in the country that requires buyers to hire an attorney for the closing. Purchasing a home is one of the biggest financial decisions you can make, and your attorney is there to review your contract before finalizing the sale, as well as assist your agent and you with anything that can arise during the homebuying process.

AGENT REPRESENTATION

Illinois is a “Designated Agency” state, which means your agent has a fiduciary responsibility to represent you and your interests. This needs to be clearly disclosed in writing, typically when you are presented a buyer agreement.

In Illinois, it is also legal for an agent to work under aDual Agency status. That occurs when an agent has both the buyer and the seller in the transaction. In this situation, both must consent to dual agency, and the agent cannot represent either party in the negotiations stage of the transactions.

PROPERTY TAXES

Property taxes are paid in arrears, meaning they are due one year after they are assessed. When you buy a home, you will typically receive a credit from the seller for unpaid taxes not yet due. This is something you’ll negotiate with the seller during the transaction.

It is also important to note that property tax rates differ based on county, city, or village, and assessments happen every 3 to 4 years depending on the location.
